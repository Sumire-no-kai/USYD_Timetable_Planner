# Product

## Register

product

## Users

University of Sydney students using Sydney Timetable during preference entry, allocation review, and post-release adjustment periods. They are trying to make practical timetable decisions quickly, often while switching between official timetable pages.

## Product Purpose

USYD Timetable Planner helps students capture timetable options, compare class times, avoid clashes, and export a planning reference. Success means the extension feels trustworthy, clear, and low-risk while making it obvious that final choices must still be confirmed in Sydney Timetable.

## Brand Personality

Practical, calm, transparent. The product should feel like a careful student-built utility: useful, honest about limits, and easy to trust without pretending to be an official university service.

## Anti-references

Avoid AI-looking landing pages, decorative gradients, broken or unnecessary logo treatment, oversized spacing, generic card grids, and marketing copy that makes the tool sound more official or more powerful than it is. Avoid bare unstyled documents that make privacy and support information hard to scan.

## Design Principles

- Make compliance information easy to verify at a glance.
- Keep the interface quiet and document-like, but not unfinished.
- Separate official limits from product features clearly.
- Prefer stable, readable layouts over promotional styling.
- Use consistent structure across homepage, privacy, and support pages.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Aim for WCAG AA contrast, keyboard-readable links and controls, responsive layouts for mobile and desktop, and no motion that is required to understand the page.
